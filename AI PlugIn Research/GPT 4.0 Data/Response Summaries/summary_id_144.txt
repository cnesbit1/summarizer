The chemical element iodine is represented by the symbol **I** and has an atomic number of 53.
In its standard state at room temperature, iodine is a solid with a distinctive dark, shiny metallic appearance and a purple vapor when it sublimates.
Chemically, it belongs to the group of halogens on the periodic table, which are known for their reactivity and tendency to form salts with metals.
