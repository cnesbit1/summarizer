Marie Curie (1867-1934) - Polish-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity, becoming the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.
Mahatma Gandhi (1869-1948) - Indian lawyer and activist who led India's independence movement against British colonial rule through nonviolent civil disobedience, inspiring movements for civil rights and freedom worldwide.
Cleopatra VII (69-30 BCE) - Last active ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt, known for her political acumen, alliances with Roman leaders like Julius Caesar and Mark Antony, and cultural influence.
Martin Luther King Jr. (1929-1968) - American Baptist minister and civil rights leader who advocated for nonviolent activism to achieve racial equality and justice, leading the American Civil Rights Movement.
Queen Elizabeth I (1533-1603) - Queen of England and Ireland known for her strong leadership during the Elizabethan Era, support of the arts and exploration, and defeating the Spanish Armada.
Confucius (551-479 BCE) - Chinese philosopher and teacher whose teachings on ethics, morality, and social harmony shaped Confucianism, a foundational philosophy in Chinese culture and governance.
These figures have left indelible marks on history through their leadership, discoveries, artistic creations, and advocacy for social change, influencing generations and shaping our understanding of the world.
