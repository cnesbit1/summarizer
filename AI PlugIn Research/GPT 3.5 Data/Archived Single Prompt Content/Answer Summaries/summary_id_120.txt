Gorillas are large primates belonging to the genus Gorilla.
They are native to Africa and are divided into two species: the eastern gorilla (Gorilla beringei) and the western gorilla (Gorilla gorilla).
These species are further divided into subspecies.
Gorillas are ground-dwelling and predominantly herbivorous, inhabiting forests and lowland swamps.
They are known for their robust build, large size, and distinctive black fur.
